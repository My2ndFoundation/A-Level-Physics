# Frontier Rules

Frontier pages exist to show where A-Level Physics ideas lead beyond the syllabus.

They are allowed, but they must remain shallow.

## Purpose

Frontier pages should provide:

- orientation
- curiosity
- conceptual map
- links back to A-Level ideas
- boundaries of what not to study yet

## Not Allowed

- university-level derivations
- dense mathematical formalism
- long technical expansions
- speculative claims presented as settled fact
- deep rabbit holes that distract from A-Level core

## Required Structure

Every frontier page must include:

- What this field studies
- Why it matters
- A-Level entry points
- Key ideas at the edge
- What not to worry about yet
- Related A-Level pages
