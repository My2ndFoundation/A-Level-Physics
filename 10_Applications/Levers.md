# Levers

## Problem Context

A lever is a rigid bar that turns about a fixed pivot (fulcrum). By applying
an effort force at one distance from the pivot, a lever can move a load at a
different distance, trading force for distance. Levers are a classic context
for the [[Principle-of-Moments]].

## Physical Ideas

- [[Moment]]
- [[Principle-of-Moments]]
- [[Force]]
- [[Centre-of-Mass]]

## Mathematical Tools

- Moment: $M = F \times d$ (perpendicular distance)
- Equilibrium condition: clockwise moments = anticlockwise moments

## Typical Questions

- Find the effort needed to lift a given load with a crowbar.
- Determine the reaction force at the pivot of a loaded beam.
- Decide where to place a pivot so a system balances.

## Method Outline

1. Draw the lever with all forces and their distances from the pivot.
2. Identify the pivot; take moments about it.
3. Set total clockwise moments equal to total anticlockwise moments.
4. Solve for the unknown effort, load, or distance.
5. If needed, use [[Resultant-Force]] $= 0$ to find the pivot reaction.

## Assumptions

- The lever is treated with the [[Rigid-Body-Model]] (it does not bend).
- The bar is light, or its weight acts at its [[Centre-of-Mass]].
- The pivot is frictionless and forces act at known points.

## Links to Other Subjects

- Mathematics: ratio and proportion, rearranging equations
- Computer Science: modelling mechanical advantage in simulations

## Frontier Links

- Biomechanics: bones and muscles as lever systems

## Common Mistakes

- Using the distance along the bar instead of the perpendicular distance.
- Ignoring the weight of the bar acting at its centre of mass.
- Forgetting the pivot exerts a reaction force found from force balance.
