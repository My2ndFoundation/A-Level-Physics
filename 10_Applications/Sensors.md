# Sensors

> Deliberately shallow **cross-subject orientation** page — how sensing connects A-Level Physics to Computer Science, not a full instrumentation course.

## Problem Context

A sensor converts a physical quantity (light, temperature, force, position) into an electrical signal. In A-Level Physics this is usually a component whose resistance changes — a thermistor or LDR — placed in a potential divider so the change becomes a measurable voltage.

## Physical Ideas

- [[Potential-Divider]]
- [[Resistance]]

## Mathematical Tools

- The potential-divider ratio; gradient/calibration relationships.

## Typical Questions

- Explain how an LDR + resistor gives an output voltage that falls with brightness.
- Choose a fixed resistor to maximise sensitivity over a range.

## Method Outline

1. The sensing component's resistance responds to the physical input.
2. A potential divider converts that resistance change to a voltage.
3. The voltage is read, logged, or fed to a logic/processing stage.

## Assumptions

- Negligible loading current from the measuring device.
- The sensor response is monotonic over the working range.

## Links to Other Subjects

- Mathematics: calibration curves, linearisation.
- Computer Science: analogue-to-digital conversion, [[Data-Logging]].

## Common Mistakes
