# Signal-Processing

> Deliberately shallow **cross-subject orientation** page — where wave/electronics physics meets Computer Science, not a full DSP course.

## Problem Context

Signal processing extracts useful information from a measured signal: filtering noise, amplifying, sampling and digitising. It rests on wave physics — a signal is a superposition of frequency components — and feeds the digital world of Computer Science.

## Physical Ideas

- [[Superposition]]
- [[Wave-Speed-Equation]]

## Mathematical Tools

- Frequency decomposition; sampling rate; basic [[Trigonometry]] of sinusoids.

## Typical Questions

- Explain why a noisy signal can be cleaned by removing certain frequency components.
- Describe how an analogue signal is sampled to become digital.

## Method Outline

1. A transducer/sensor produces an analogue electrical signal.
2. Filtering and amplification condition it.
3. Sampling + quantisation convert it to digital data for a computer.

## Assumptions

- Linear, time-invariant behaviour at A-Level depth.
- Sampling fast enough to capture the signal's highest useful frequency.

## Links to Other Subjects

- Mathematics: superposition of sinusoids, Fourier idea (orientation only).
- Computer Science: sampling, digitisation, [[Data-Logging]].

## Frontier Links

- [[Semiconductor-Physics-Map]]

## Common Mistakes

- Thinking a complex signal has a single frequency rather than a spectrum.
