Navigation for the GCSE-foundation layer. Syllabus structure from

prerequisite feeding the bridge-to-A-Level pages and [[OCR-Physics-Map]].

teaching content is ingested.

## GCSE Syllabus Structure (OCR J249, P1–P9)

- **P1 Matter** — P1.1 the particle model · P1.2 changes of state · P1.3 pressure
- **P2 Forces** — P2.1 motion · P2.2 Newton's laws · P2.3 forces in action
- **P3 Electricity** — P3.1 static and charge · P3.2 simple circuits
- **P4 Magnetism and magnetic fields** — P4.1 magnets and magnetic fields · P4.2 uses of magnetism
- **P5 Waves in matter** — P5.1 wave behaviour · P5.2 the electromagnetic spectrum · P5.3 wave interaction
- **P6 Radioactivity** — P6.1 radioactive emissions · P6.2 uses and hazards
- **P7 Energy** — P7.1 work done · P7.2 power and efficiency
- **P8 Global challenges** — P8.1 physics on the move · P8.2 powering Earth · P8.3 beyond Earth
- **P9 Practical skills** (skills topic)

### Assessment

- Foundation Tier — Paper 1 (J249/01) + Paper 2 (J249/02)
- Higher Tier — Paper 3 (J249/03) + Paper 4 (J249/04)
- Each paper: 90 marks · 1 h 45 min · 50% of GCSE
- Papers 1/3 → P1–P4 + P9; Papers 2/4 → P5–P8 (assumed knowledge of P1–P4 + P9)
