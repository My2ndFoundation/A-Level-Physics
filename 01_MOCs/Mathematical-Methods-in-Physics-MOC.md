# Mathematical Methods in Physics MOC

## OCR Mathematical Skills Framework (M0–M4)

Maths-skills alignment for OCR Physics A H556 (spec §5e), as defined in the
[[OCR-Physics-Mathematical-Skills-Handbook]]. The spec's additional-guidance
column tags content with these M-codes. Descriptors are brief paraphrased
anchors; atomic `method` / `representation` pages link in here as content
sources teach each skill.

- **M0 — Arithmetic and numerical computation**
  - M0.1 appropriate units in calculations
  - M0.2 decimal and standard form
  - M0.3 ratios, fractions and percentages
  - M0.4 estimating results
  - M0.5 calculator: power, exponential and logarithm functions
  - M0.6 calculator: sin/cos/tan in radians or degrees
- **M1 — Handling data**
  - M1.1 appropriate significant figures
  - M1.2 arithmetic means
  - M1.3 simple probability
  - M1.4 order-of-magnitude calculations
  - M1.5 uncertainties → [[Practical-Skills-MOC]]
- **M2 — Algebra**
  - M2.1 symbols = < ≪ > ≫ ∝ ≈ ∆
  - M2.2 changing the subject (including non-linear equations)
  - M2.3 substituting numerical values with appropriate units
  - M2.4 solving algebraic equations, including quadratics
  - M2.5 logarithms across several orders of magnitude
- **M3 — Graphs**
  - M3.1 translate between graphical, numerical and algebraic forms
  - M3.2 plot two variables from data
  - M3.3 y = mx + c as a linear relationship
  - M3.4 slope and intercept of a linear graph
  - M3.5 rate of change from a linear graph
  - M3.6 slope of a tangent as a rate of change
  - M3.7 instantaneous vs average rate of change
  - M3.8 physical significance of area between curve and x-axis
  - M3.9 calculus contexts via graphical/spreadsheet methods (no explicit derivatives/integrals)
  - M3.10 interpret logarithmic plots
  - M3.11 log plots for exponential and power-law relationships
  - M3.12 sketch relationships
- **M4 — Geometry and trigonometry**
  - M4.1 angles in 2D and 3D structures
  - M4.2 visualise and represent 2D and 3D forms
  - M4.3 areas and volumes (triangles, circles, blocks, cylinders, spheres)
  - M4.4 Pythagoras's theorem and the angle sum of a triangle
  - M4.5 sin/cos/tan in physical problems
  - M4.6 small-angle approximations
  - M4.7 degrees ↔ radians
