# Resolution, Accuracy and Precision

## Core Idea

Four practical-skills terms with distinct meanings: **resolution** (smallest change an instrument can show), **accuracy** (closeness to the true value), **precision** (closeness of repeats to each other), and **repeatability/reproducibility** (consistency on repeating).

## Meaning

- **Resolution** — the finest increment readable: a metre rule resolves to 1 mm, a digital balance to its last displayed digit. Resolution sets a floor on [[Measurement-Uncertainty]] but does not guarantee accuracy.
- **Accuracy** — how close a result is to the true value. Poor accuracy usually signals a [[Systematic-and-Random-Errors|systematic error]].
- **Precision** — how tightly repeated readings cluster, regardless of whether they are correct. Poor precision signals large [[Systematic-and-Random-Errors|random error]].
- **Repeatability** — same operator, same equipment, consistent results. **Reproducibility** — consistent results when someone else, or different equipment, repeats it.

A high-resolution instrument can still be inaccurate (zero error) or imprecise (noisy). The classic picture: tight grouping off-target = precise but inaccurate; spread around the bullseye = accurate on average but imprecise.

## Everyday Intuition

A stopwatch reading to 0.01 s has fine resolution, but if you start it late every time it is still inaccurate.

## Why It Matters

These words are used precisely in exam mark schemes and evaluations. Misusing "accurate" for "precise" loses marks and hides whether the fix is *average more* or *redesign the method*.

```mermaid
quadrantChart
    title Accuracy vs Precision
    x-axis Low Accuracy --> High Accuracy
    y-axis Low Precision --> High Precision
    quadrant-1 Precise AND Accurate (ideal)
    quadrant-2 Precise but Inaccurate (systematic error)
    quadrant-3 Neither (large random + systematic error)
    quadrant-4 Accurate on average but Imprecise (large random error)
```
*Figure: Four categories of measurement quality. Resolution sets the finest readable increment but does not guarantee accuracy. Systematic error → inaccuracy; random error → imprecision.*
