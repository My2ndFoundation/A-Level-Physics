# Capacitor

## Core Idea

A capacitor is a component that stores energy by separating positive and negative charge across an insulating gap, producing an electric field between two conductors.

## Meaning

A capacitor in its simplest form is two conducting plates separated by an insulator (the dielectric). When connected to a source of [[Potential-Difference]], electrons are pushed onto one plate and pulled off the other. One plate gains charge +Q, the other −Q, while the net charge stays zero. The separated charge sets up an [[Electric-Field]] between the plates and stores [[Energy]].

The defining relationship is the [[Capacitance]] equation:

- Q = C V
  - Q = magnitude of charge on each plate, coulombs (C)
  - V = potential difference across the plates, volts (V)
  - C = capacitance, farads (F)

Capacitance C is fixed by geometry and the dielectric, so Q is directly proportional to V. A graph of Q against V is a straight line through the origin with gradient C.

For a parallel-plate capacitor, C = εA / d, where A is plate area, d is plate separation, and ε is the permittivity of the dielectric. Larger area or a thinner gap increases capacitance.

## Everyday Intuition

A capacitor behaves like a tiny rechargeable store: charge flows in quickly, builds up a "pressure" (voltage), then can be released rapidly. The flash unit in a camera charges slowly from a small battery, then dumps energy into the bulb almost instantly.

## Why It Matters

Capacitors smooth rectified voltages, provide timing in [[Capacitor-Timing-Circuits]], store energy for rapid release (camera flash, defibrillator), and block DC while passing AC. Charging and discharging follow exponential behaviour described by the [[Capacitor-Discharge-Equation]].

## Common Mistakes

- Thinking charge crosses the dielectric (no charge flows through an ideal capacitor; current only flows in the connecting wires).
- Confusing the charge Q on one plate with total charge (net charge is zero).
- Treating C as variable with voltage (C is fixed by geometry; Q changes, not C).
