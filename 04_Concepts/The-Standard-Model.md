# The Standard Model

## Core Idea

The Standard Model is the framework that organises all known fundamental matter particles (quarks and leptons) and the particles that mediate their interactions.

## Meaning

At A-Level depth the Standard Model is treated as a **classification scheme**, not as a quantum field theory. The matter content is:

- **Quarks** (6): up, down, charm, strange, top, bottom — feel the strong interaction, bind into [[Fundamental-Particles|hadrons]].
- **Leptons** (6): electron, muon, tau, and three neutrinos — do not feel the strong interaction.
- Every particle has a corresponding [[Antiparticles|antiparticle]].

Composite particles classify as:

- **Hadrons** (feel the strong interaction): **baryons** (3 quarks, e.g. proton, neutron) and **mesons** (quark–antiquark pairs, e.g. pions, kaons).

Interactions are carried by exchange particles (the photon mediates electromagnetism; the weak interaction governs beta decay and quark flavour change). The model is supported by enormous experimental evidence, including the discovery of predicted particles.

> Out of scope at A-Level: quantum field theory, gauge symmetry, Higgs mechanism mathematics, and full interaction Feynman-diagram calculations. Treat these as orientation only.

## Everyday Intuition

The Standard Model is the periodic table of particle physics: a compact chart from which all observed particles can be assembled.
