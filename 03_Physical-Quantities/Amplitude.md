# Amplitude

## Core Idea

Amplitude is the size of an oscillation вЂ” the maximum displacement from the equilibrium (rest) position. A loud sound has a large pressure amplitude; a bright light has a large electric-field amplitude. It controls how much energy a wave or oscillation carries, not how fast it repeats.

Read the peak value on an oscilloscope or data-logger trace, or measure the maximum displacement directly (e.g. with a scale behind an oscillating mass or a CRO for a sound signal). Averaging over several cycles improves reliability.

## Common Mistakes

- Taking the full peak-to-trough distance as the amplitude (it is half of that)
- Confusing amplitude with wavelength
- Assuming amplitude affects frequency or period (it does not, for SHM)
