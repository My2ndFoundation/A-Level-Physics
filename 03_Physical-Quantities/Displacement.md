# Displacement

## Core Idea

Displacement is how far and in what direction an object has moved from its starting point. It is the straight-line "shortcut" from start to finish, not the length of the path actually travelled.

## Symbol

`s` (sometimes `x` or `r`)

## SI Unit

`m` (metre)

## Scalar or Vector

Vector. It has both magnitude and direction. The displacement of a complete round trip is zero, even though the distance travelled is not.

## Definition

Displacement is the change in position of an object, measured as a vector from the initial position to the final position.

## Related Equations

- `Δs = s_final − s_initial` — positions measured from a chosen origin (m).
- SUVAT: `s = ut + ½at²` — `s` = displacement (m), `u` = initial velocity (m s⁻¹), `a` = acceleration (m s⁻²), `t` = time (s).
- `s = v̄ t` — `v̄` = average velocity (m s⁻¹).
- Resultant of two perpendicular displacements: `s = √(s_x² + s_y²)`.

## How It Is Measured

Measured with rulers, tape measures, motion sensors, or by tracking position from video analysis, always relative to a defined origin and direction. Vector addition is used when motion has components in more than one direction.

## Graphical Meaning

- On a displacement–time graph, the **gradient** is velocity.
- On a [[Velocity-Time-Graph]], the **area under the curve** is displacement (areas below the axis count as negative).
