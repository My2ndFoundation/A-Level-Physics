# Angular Velocity

## Core Idea

Angular velocity measures how fast an object rotates or sweeps out angle around a centre — the rate of change of angular position.

## Symbol

- ω (omega)

## SI Unit

- radian per second (rad s⁻¹). The radian is dimensionless, so the base-unit form is s⁻¹.

## Scalar or Vector

- Treated as a vector along the rotation axis at A-Level it is usually handled as a magnitude (angular speed). Its sense (clockwise/anticlockwise) carries the directional information.

## Definition

Angular velocity is the angle swept per unit time:

$$\omega = \Delta\theta / \Delta t$$

For uniform [[Circular-Motion]] this is constant. It relates to linear (tangential) speed v and radius r by:

$$v = \omega r$$

and to the [[Period]] T and [[Frequency]] f of one revolution by:

$$\omega = 2\pi / T = 2\pi f$$

## Related Equations

- $\omega = \Delta\theta / \Delta t$
- $v = \omega r$
- $\omega = 2\pi f = 2\pi / T$
- [[Centripetal-Acceleration]]: $a = \omega^2 r = v^2 / r$

## How It Is Measured

Measure the time for a known whole number of revolutions (e.g. 10) with a stopwatch and divide total angle (10 × 2π rad) by total time; averaging over many turns reduces timing uncertainty. A light gate plus rotating card, or a video frame analysis, gives Δθ over a measured Δt directly.

## Graphical Meaning

On an angular displacement θ against time t graph, the gradient is the angular velocity ω. For uniform rotation the graph is a straight line of constant gradient; a curved line indicates angular acceleration.
