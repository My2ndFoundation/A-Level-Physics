# Distance

## Core Idea

Distance is how much ground an object has covered along its actual path, regardless of direction. Walking 3 m forward then 3 m back gives a distance of 6 m but a displacement of zero.

## Symbol

`d` (sometimes `s` for path length)

## SI Unit

`m` (metre)

## Scalar or Vector

Scalar. It has magnitude only and can never be negative or decrease.

## Definition

Distance is the total length of the path travelled by an object between two instants, independent of direction.

## Related Equations

- `distance = speed × time` → `d = v̄ t` — `v̄` = average speed (m s⁻¹), `t` = time (s).
- For a curved path, distance is the total arc length travelled.
- Distance ≥ |displacement| always.

## How It Is Measured

Measured with rulers, tape measures, trundle wheels, or odometers, or computed by summing successive small path segments from sensor or video data.

## Graphical Meaning

On a speed–time graph the **area under the curve** gives distance travelled (all areas count as positive, since speed is never negative). The gradient of a distance–time graph gives speed.
