# Conservation of Momentum Collision

## Problem Signal

Two bodies interact (collide, stick together, or push apart in an explosion/recoil) with no significant external force during the interaction. The question asks for an unknown velocity, mass, or energy lost. Trigger phrases: "collide and move off together", "perfectly elastic", "recoil", "explosion", "two trolleys", "bullet embeds in block".

- [[Momentum]]

- Isolated system

## Standard Approach

1. Define a positive direction and the system (which bodies are included).
2. Write total momentum before = total momentum after: $\Sigma m u = \Sigma m v$, keeping vector signs.
3. For "stick together", the combined mass moves with one common final velocity.
4. Solve for the unknown velocity or mass.
5. If asked about elasticity, compare total kinetic energy before and after; equal means elastic.
6. For an explosion, total initial momentum is often zero, so final momenta are equal and opposite.

## Variations

- 2D collision: conserve momentum independently along two perpendicular axes.
- Elastic collision: use both momentum and kinetic-energy conservation simultaneously.
- Recoil (gun/rocket): zero initial momentum gives $m_1 v_1 = -m_2 v_2$.

## Common Traps

- [[Forgetting-Vector-Direction]]
- Assuming kinetic energy is conserved in every collision (only elastic ones).
- Forgetting that momentum is conserved even when kinetic energy is not.
- Dropping the sign of a velocity moving in the negative direction.
