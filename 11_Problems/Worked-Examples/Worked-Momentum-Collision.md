# Worked Momentum Collision

## Problem

A trolley of mass $1.2\ \text{kg}$ moves to the right at $3.0\ \text{m s}^{-1}$ and collides head-on with a stationary trolley of mass $0.80\ \text{kg}$. After the collision the two trolleys move off **together**. Find (a) their common velocity after the collision, and (b) the kinetic energy lost in the collision, stating whether it is elastic.

## Solution

**Set up.** Take rightward as positive. System: both trolleys (external forces negligible during the brief collision).

**(a) Common velocity.** Conserve momentum:

$$m_1 u_1 + m_2 u_2 = (m_1 + m_2)v$$
$$(1.2)(3.0) + (0.80)(0) = (1.2 + 0.80)\,v$$
$$3.6 = 2.0\,v \implies v = 1.8\ \text{m s}^{-1}\ \text{(rightward)}$$

**(b) Kinetic energy change.**

Before: $E_{k,i} = \tfrac{1}{2}(1.2)(3.0)^2 = 5.4\ \text{J}$.

After: $E_{k,f} = \tfrac{1}{2}(2.0)(1.8)^2 = \tfrac{1}{2}(2.0)(3.24) = 3.24\ \text{J}$.

Kinetic energy lost $= 5.4 - 3.24 = 2.2\ \text{J}$.

Since kinetic energy is not conserved, the collision is **inelastic** (consistent with the trolleys sticking together).

## Unit Check

Momentum: $(\text{kg})(\text{m s}^{-1}) = \text{kg m s}^{-1}$ on both sides. Energy: $(\text{kg})(\text{m s}^{-1})^2 = \text{kg m}^2\text{s}^{-2} = \text{J}$. Consistent.

## Key Insight

Momentum is always conserved for an isolated system, but kinetic energy is conserved only in elastic collisions. A "stick together" outcome is the maximally inelastic case.
