# Mixing Up Series and Parallel Circuits

## Mistake

Applying series rules to a parallel arrangement (or vice versa) — for example adding resistances directly when they are in parallel, or assuming equal current through parallel branches.

## Why It Happens

The two rule sets are introduced close together and look similar. Without redrawing the circuit clearly, learners pattern-match on appearance rather than tracing the actual connections.

## Example

Two 6 Ω resistors are connected in parallel. A student gives the combined resistance as 6 + 6 = 12 Ω. For two equal resistors in parallel the combined resistance is 3 Ω, half of one resistor's value.

## Correct Approach

Redraw the circuit and identify which components share the same current (series) and which share the same potential difference (parallel). Add resistances directly only for series; for parallel, combine reciprocals. Check that total resistance falls when adding a parallel branch.
