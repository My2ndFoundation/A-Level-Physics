# Mixing Speed and Velocity

## Mistake

Treating speed and velocity as the same quantity, so a change of direction at constant speed is wrongly described as "no change," or velocity answers are given without direction.

## Why It Happens

In straight-line motion the numerical values match, and everyday speech uses "speed" for both. The vector nature of velocity is easy to overlook when only a number is asked for.

## Example

A car travels round a roundabout at a steady 30 km/h. A student says it is not accelerating because its speed is constant. The velocity direction is continuously changing, so the car *is* accelerating even though its speed is fixed.

## Correct Approach

Remember speed is a scalar (magnitude only) and velocity is a vector (magnitude and direction). Any change in direction is a change in velocity, hence an acceleration. State a direction whenever velocity is reported.
