# Treating Scalars as Vectors

## Mistake

Assigning a direction to a scalar quantity, or trying to resolve scalars into components — for example resolving energy, mass, time or speed as if they had direction.

## Why It Happens

The component method is drilled heavily for forces and velocities, so learners over-generalise it and apply it to any quantity in a problem, including scalars.

## Example

To find total kinetic energy of two objects moving at right angles, a student resolves each object's kinetic energy into x and y "components" and uses Pythagoras. Kinetic energy is a scalar; the total is simply the arithmetic sum of the individual kinetic energies.

## Correct Approach

Classify each quantity first. Scalars (mass, time, energy, speed, temperature) add arithmetically and have no direction. Only vectors (force, velocity, displacement, momentum) are resolved and combined with vector methods.
