# Treating Centripetal Force as an Extra Force

## Mistake

Adding a separate "centripetal force" arrow on a free-body diagram, in
addition to the real forces, as if it were a new force in its own right.

## Why It Happens

The phrase "centripetal force" sounds like a named force such as gravity or
friction. In fact it is the *name for the net inward force* — the role played
by whichever real forces point towards the centre. It is a result, not an
extra cause.

## Example

A ball on a string is whirled in a horizontal circle. A student draws weight,
tension, *and* an extra "centripetal force" pointing inward. There is no
fourth force: the horizontal component of the [[Centripetal-Force]] role is
already supplied by the tension. Counting it twice doubles the inward force.

## Correct Approach

Draw only the real forces. Then identify their resultant towards the centre
and set it equal to $mv^2/r$. The centripetal requirement is what the [[Resultant-Force]]
must equal, not an additional arrow.

## Foundation Link

It stems from confusing the *description* of motion with its *causes* — the
same root as misreading [[Newton-First-Law]].
