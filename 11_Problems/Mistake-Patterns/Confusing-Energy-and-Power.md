# Confusing Energy and Power

## Mistake

Treating energy and power as interchangeable — quoting energy in watts, power in joules, or ignoring the time over which energy is transferred.

## Why It Happens

Everyday usage blurs the terms ("a powerful battery"), and both appear in similar equations, so the role of time as the distinguishing factor is overlooked.

## Example

A motor transfers 6000 J in 30 s. A student states its power as 6000 W. Power is energy transferred per second, so the correct power is 6000 ÷ 30 = 200 W.

## Correct Approach

Keep the definitions distinct: energy is measured in joules; power is the rate of energy transfer in watts (joules per second). When a rate is involved, divide energy by time. Check units to confirm whether the quantity is an energy or a power.
