# Ignoring Units

## Mistake

Substituting numbers into an equation without converting to consistent SI units, or omitting the unit from the final answer.

## Why It Happens

Calculators only handle numbers, so attention drifts to digits. Mixed prefixes (cm, kN, ms, μF) make it easy to substitute raw figures and lose track of scale.

## Example

A student finds the extension of a wire using a length given as 200 cm and a force in newtons, leaving the length in centimetres. Mixing centimetres with SI quantities makes the result wrong by a factor of 100; the length must first be converted to 2.00 m.

## Correct Approach

Convert every quantity to base SI units before substituting. Track units through the calculation and confirm the result's unit matches the expected quantity. A unit check is a fast way to detect a setup error.
