# From Speed to Velocity

## Core Idea

GCSE often starts with speed. A-Level Physics needs velocity because direction matters.

## Prior Knowledge Level

Bridge to A-Level

## Meaning

Speed is scalar. Velocity is vector. Two objects can have the same speed but different velocities if they move in different directions.

## Why It Matters for A-Level Physics

Velocity is essential for acceleration, momentum, circular motion, waves, and vector-based mechanics.

## Everyday Intuition

A car going north at 30 mph and a car going south at 30 mph have the same speed but different velocities.
