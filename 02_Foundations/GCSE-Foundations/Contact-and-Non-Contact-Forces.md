# Contact and Non-Contact Forces

## Core Idea

A contact force acts only when two objects are physically touching. A
non-contact force acts at a distance, through a field, without the objects
touching.

## Prior Knowledge Level

- GCSE

## Meaning

Contact forces include friction, the normal (support) force, air resistance,
tension in a rope, and a push or pull by hand. They appear only at the surface
where objects meet.

Non-contact forces include gravitational force (weight), electrostatic force,
and magnetic force. They are explained using the idea of a *field* — a region
in which an object experiences a force without being touched.

## Why It Matters for A-Level Physics

The non-contact group becomes the A-Level study of gravitational, electric,
and magnetic *fields*. The contact group underlies [[Friction]], normal
reaction, and most free-body diagrams used with [[Newton-Second-Law]].

## Everyday Intuition

Kicking a ball is a contact force. A dropped ball falling to the ground is
pulled by gravity — a non-contact force acting even though nothing touches it.

## Common Misconceptions

- Believing gravity needs air or some medium to act (it acts in a vacuum).
- Thinking magnetism only works on touching objects.

## Bridge to A-Level

At A-Level "non-contact force" is made precise through field strength
(force per unit mass or per unit charge) and field lines, while contact forces
are decomposed into normal and frictional components in equilibrium and
dynamics problems.
