# Using a Micrometer

## Aim

To measure a small length — typically the diameter of a wire or the thickness of a thin sample — to a resolution of 0.01 mm.

## Variables

- Independent variable: not applicable — this is a measurement technique used inside other experiments.
- Dependent variable: the small length being measured (e.g. wire diameter `d`).
- Control variables: same sample, same temperature, gauge not over-tightened (use the ratchet).

## Apparatus

- Micrometer screw gauge (resolution 0.01 mm), with a ratchet for consistent closing force.
- The sample to be measured; a soft cloth to keep faces clean.

## Method

1. Check the **zero error**: close the jaws fully (using the ratchet) and read the scale. Record any non-zero reading to subtract later.
2. Open the gauge and place the sample between the anvil and spindle.
3. Close the spindle using the **ratchet** until it clicks — this applies a standard force and avoids crushing the sample.
4. Read the main scale (whole and half millimetres) at the edge of the thimble.
5. Add the thimble scale reading (each division = 0.01 mm) aligned with the datum line.
6. Subtract the zero error to obtain the true measurement.
7. Repeat at several positions and orientations along the sample and take a mean.

## Measurements

Main scale reading + thimble scale reading, corrected for zero error, in millimetres; repeated readings for a mean.

## Data Processing

Mean of repeated readings (taken at different points to average out non-uniformity). Convert to metres for use in calculations such as cross-sectional area $A = \frac{\pi d^2}{4}$.

## Graph Use

Not directly graphical, but the result feeds quantities like [[Stress]] (via area) into a [[Stress-Strain-Graph]] or [[Force-Extension-Graph]].

## Uncertainty

- Resolution uncertainty: ±0.005 mm (half the 0.01 mm division), or the spread of repeats if larger.
- Sources: zero error (systematic), over-tightening, non-uniform sample, dirty faces.
- Reduction: always use the ratchet, check and subtract zero error, repeat at several places and orientations, keep faces clean.

## Safety / Practical Limits

Low risk. Do not over-tighten — it damages both gauge and sample and biases the reading. Suitable only for samples up to ~25 mm.

## Common Mistakes

- Ignoring zero error.
- Tightening with the thimble instead of the ratchet, crushing the wire.
- Taking only one reading on a non-uniform wire.
- Misreading the half-millimetre mark on the main scale.
