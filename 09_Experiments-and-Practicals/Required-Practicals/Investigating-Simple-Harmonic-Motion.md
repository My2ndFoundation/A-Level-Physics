# Investigating Simple Harmonic Motion

## Aim

To investigate how the [[Period]] of a simple harmonic oscillator depends on its physical parameters — verifying T = 2π√(m/k) for a mass–spring system and T = 2π√(L/g) for a simple pendulum — and to determine g.

## Variables

- Independent variable: suspended mass m (mass–spring) or pendulum length L (pendulum).
- Dependent variable: period T (timed over many oscillations).
- Control variables: spring stiffness k (same spring), [[Amplitude]] kept small and constant, same location (g), no draughts.

## Apparatus

- Spring, slotted masses and hanger, clamp stand and boss
- Stopwatch (or light gate + datalogger)
- Metre rule, fiducial marker
- For pendulum: bob, inextensible thread, split cork or clamp; protractor for small angle (< ~10°)

## Method

1. Set up the oscillator and a fixed reference (fiducial) marker at the equilibrium position.
2. Displace by a small amplitude and release; let one or two oscillations pass before timing.
3. Time 10 (or 20) complete oscillations and divide by the number to get T; repeat and average.
4. Change the independent variable (add mass, or alter pendulum length) and repeat over a sensible range (typically 6–8 values).
5. Record m or L and the corresponding mean T.

## Measurements

Mean period T from total time ÷ number of oscillations; mass m on a balance; length L (to centre of bob) with a metre rule.

## Data Processing

Square the SHM period relations to get straight lines:

- Mass–spring: T² = (4π²/k) m — plot T² (y) against m (x).
- Pendulum: T² = (4π²/g) L — plot T² (y) against L (x).

## Graph Use

For the pendulum, T² against L is a straight line through the origin with gradient 4π²/g, so g = 4π² / gradient. For the mass–spring, gradient of T² against m is 4π²/k, giving k; a non-zero T²-intercept reveals the effective mass of the spring. A straight line confirms T² ∝ m (or L), supporting the [[Simple-Harmonic-Motion-Equation]].

## Uncertainty

- Reaction-time error on timing — reduced by timing many oscillations and using a fiducial marker at the fastest point (equilibrium), or a light gate.
- Length measurement (to centre of bob) and amplitude not kept small — keep angle < 10° so sin θ ≈ θ ([[Radian]]).
- [[Damping]] reduces amplitude over a run — keep runs short; air resistance assumed negligible.
- Repeat readings and average; quote percentage uncertainties.

## Safety / Practical Limits

Secure clamp stand with a counterweight so falling masses cannot topple it; keep feet clear of falling masses; do not over-stretch the spring beyond its elastic limit ([[Hookes-Law]] must still hold).
