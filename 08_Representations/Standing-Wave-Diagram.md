# Standing-Wave Diagram

## Core Idea

A standing-wave diagram represents a [[Standing-Waves|stationary wave]] by drawing the envelope of its oscillation, marking the fixed nodes and the maximally oscillating antinodes. It shows the allowed patterns (harmonics) on a string, in a pipe, or in a microwave cavity.

## Form

The string or air column is drawn with the wave shown at its two extreme positions, producing a lens/loop shape between each pair of nodes. Nodes are points of zero displacement marked on the axis; antinodes are the midpoints of the loops where displacement is greatest. Successive harmonics are drawn with increasing numbers of loops.

## Axes / Labels / Components

Not a time graph — a spatial snapshot of the oscillation envelope:

- Horizontal axis: position along the string or pipe.
- The two extreme waveforms (dashed) and the envelope between them.
- Nodes (N): zero-amplitude points, spaced half a wavelength apart.
- Antinodes (A): maximum-amplitude points, midway between nodes.
- Boundary conditions: fixed ends are nodes; an open pipe end is an antinode.

## Physical Meaning

The pattern forms from two identical waves travelling in opposite directions superposing. The distance between adjacent nodes (or adjacent antinodes) is `λ/2`, so the diagram lets you read off the wavelength from the physical length. The fundamental has the fewest loops; each higher harmonic adds a loop and a node.

## Gradient / Area / Intercepts

Not applicable. The key measurable is the node-to-node spacing = half a wavelength, used with $v = f\lambda$ to find wave speed.

## Converts To / From

- From: an [[Investigating-Stationary-Waves]] experiment (string under tension, Melde's apparatus, resonance tube).
- To: a wavelength value (from node spacing) and hence frequency or wave speed.

## Related Quantities

- _Wavelength, frequency, tension (wave quantities)._

## Related Methods

- Measuring node-to-node distance as λ/2
- Counting harmonics

## Common Mistakes

- Confusing nodes (zero displacement) with antinodes (maximum displacement).
- Taking node-to-node distance as a full wavelength rather than half.
- Forgetting that an open pipe end is an antinode while a closed/fixed end is a node.
