# Magnetic Field Line Diagram

## Core Idea

A magnetic field line diagram shows the shape, direction, and relative strength of a [[Magnetic-Field]] using continuous directed lines.

## Form

Smooth curved lines with arrowheads. They emerge from a north pole and enter a south pole (outside a magnet), forming closed loops that continue south→north inside the magnet. For a long straight wire the lines are concentric circles around it; for a solenoid they resemble a bar magnet's field with a nearly uniform field inside.

## Axes / Labels / Components

Not a graph, so there are no axes. Components: the source (bar magnet, wire, solenoid, or pole pieces), directed field lines, pole labels (N, S), and the symbols "×" (field into the page) and "•" or dot (field out of the page) for fields perpendicular to the diagram.

## Physical Meaning

The tangent to a line at any point gives the direction of **B** there (the way a compass north would point). The relative density of lines shows field strength: closely spaced lines mean a strong field, widely spaced lines a weak field. Lines never cross, because the field has a single well-defined direction at each point. Parallel, evenly spaced lines indicate a uniform field (e.g. between flat magnet poles or inside a long solenoid).

## Gradient / Area / Intercepts

Not applicable in the graph sense. The analogous readable feature is **line spacing**: converging lines indicate increasing [[Magnetic-Flux-Density]]; uniform spacing indicates a uniform field used in [[Force-on-a-Current-Carrying-Conductor]] and [[Force-on-a-Moving-Charge]] problems.

## Converts To / From

- From: a physical field measured with a plotting compass, iron filings, or a Hall probe.
- To: numerical values via [[Magnetic-Flux]] $\Phi = B A \cos\theta$ over a chosen area.

## Common Mistakes

- Drawing field lines that cross or stop in mid-space (they must be continuous and non-intersecting).
- Reversing the convention (lines run N→S *outside* the magnet).
- Ignoring spacing, so treating a non-uniform field as uniform.
- Confusing "×" (into page) and "•" (out of page).
