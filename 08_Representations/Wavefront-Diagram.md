# Wavefront Diagram

## Core Idea

A wavefront diagram represents a wave by drawing lines (wavefronts) that join points oscillating in phase. It complements the [[Ray-Diagram]] by showing the wave's spatial structure rather than just its direction.

## Form

Wavefronts are drawn as a series of parallel lines (plane wave) or concentric circles/arcs (circular or spherical wave). Successive wavefronts are one wavelength apart and represent, for example, successive crests. Rays, when added, are perpendicular to the wavefronts and show the direction of energy travel.

## Axes / Labels / Components

Not a graph. Components:

- Wavefronts: lines of constant phase, spacing = wavelength `λ`.
- Direction of propagation: an arrow perpendicular to the wavefronts.
- Source: the point or line from which circular/plane fronts spread.
- At a boundary or gap: incident, refracted and diffracted wavefronts.

## Physical Meaning

The spacing between wavefronts is the wavelength; closer fronts mean a shorter wavelength. When a wave slows on entering a medium the wavefronts bunch closer and change direction (refraction). When wavefronts pass through a gap comparable to `λ` they curve at the edges, directly visualising diffraction. Overlapping wavefront sets show where constructive and destructive interference occur.

## Gradient / Area / Intercepts

Not applicable. The geometry conveys wavelength (front spacing), speed change (spacing change at a boundary), and diffraction/interference (curving and overlap of fronts).

## Converts To / From

- From: a wave scenario (ripple tank, sound, light through slits).
- To: a [[Ray-Diagram]] (rays drawn perpendicular to the fronts) and predictions of refraction and diffraction.

## Related Quantities

- _Wavelength, wave speed, frequency (wave quantities)._

## Related Methods

- Constructing rays perpendicular to wavefronts
- Analysing path difference for interference

## Common Mistakes

- Drawing wavefront spacing that does not equal one wavelength.
- Forgetting that wavefronts bunch (not spread) when a wave slows in a denser medium.
- Drawing rays parallel to wavefronts instead of perpendicular.
