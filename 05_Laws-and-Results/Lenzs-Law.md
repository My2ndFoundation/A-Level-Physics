# Lenzs Law

## Statement

The direction of an induced current (or e.m.f.) is always such that it opposes the change in magnetic flux that produced it.

## Equation

Lenz's law is expressed by the minus sign in Faraday's law:

ε = −d(NΦ)/dt

## Symbols and Units

- Symbol: ε — Meaning: induced e.m.f. — Unit: volt (V)
- Symbol: NΦ — Meaning: [[Magnetic-Flux-Linkage]] — Unit: weber (Wb)
- Symbol: t — Meaning: time — Unit: second (s)
- The minus sign carries no unit; it encodes direction/opposition.

## Conditions

- Applies whenever flux linkage changes and a closed conducting path exists for an induced current.
- Determines only the *direction* of the induced effect; magnitude comes from [[Faradays-Law]].

## Physical Meaning

If a magnet's north pole approaches a coil, the induced current flows so the near face of the coil becomes a north pole, repelling the magnet and opposing the approach. If the magnet is withdrawn, the induced current reverses to attract it, opposing the withdrawal. Either way, the induced current resists the change. This is a direct statement of [[Conservation-of-Energy]]: if the induced current aided the change, motion would amplify itself and create energy from nothing. Work done against the opposing force is what supplies the electrical energy.

## Foundation Link

Builds on the GCSE idea that a generator becomes "harder to turn" when its output drives a current — the opposition learners can feel directly.

## How to Use

1. Decide whether flux through the coil is increasing or decreasing.
2. The induced current opposes that change (opposes an increase, sustains a decrease).
3. Use the right-hand grip rule to find the induced current direction that produces the opposing field.

## Derivation or Explanation

Not derived independently — it is the consequence of energy conservation applied to electromagnetic induction, and is built into the sign convention of [[Faradays-Law]].

## Common Mistakes

- Getting the induced-current direction backwards (forgetting it *opposes* the change).
- Thinking Lenz's law gives the size of the e.m.f. (it gives only direction).
- Believing it violates energy conservation, when it is in fact a statement of it.
