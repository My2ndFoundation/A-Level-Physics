# Newton Second Law

## Statement

The resultant force on an object is equal to the rate of change of momentum. For constant mass, this is commonly written as `F = ma`.

## Equation

`F = ma`

## Symbols and Units

- `F`: resultant force, newtons `N`
- `m`: mass, kilograms `kg`
- `a`: acceleration, metres per second squared `m s⁻²`

## Conditions

The simplified form `F = ma` assumes constant mass.

## Physical Meaning

A resultant force causes acceleration in the direction of the resultant force.

## Foundation Link

This builds on the GCSE idea of force as a push or pull and makes it quantitative.

## How to Use

Use the resultant force, not just any single force.
